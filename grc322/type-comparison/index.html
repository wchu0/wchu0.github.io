Clarendon vs Sutro

History

Clarendon

  • Created by Robert Besley in England in 1845, for the Fann Street Foundry

  • Known as the first registered typeface

Sutro

  • Sutro was created by Jim Parkinson in 2003

Comparisons

Similarities

  • Both typefaces feature serifs

  • Left side stems and crossbars tend to be thin

  • Bowls thin out when they near the stem

  • Terminals curve towards the stem

  • Numbers four and eight share similar variations in line width and form

  • Similar in form but vary in width

Differences

  • Clarendon’s serifs are rounded, while Sutro’s serifs are right angles

  • Some of Sutro’s serifs are angled at the tip, while all of Clarendon’s are flat

  • Clarendon’s terminals are circular, while Sutro’s remain thicker lines

  • Numerals differ in Clarendon's rounded terminals vs Sutro's flat edges

  • Sutro's '7' has a straight-edge top bar whereas Clarendon's has a stylized, wavy top bar

Examples

  • Clarendon typeface used on Rock Creek's welcome sign

  • Clarendon typeface used for Wells Fargo's logo

  • Sutro typeface used to design the Sutro-calendar for ADAC 38th season
